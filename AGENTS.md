# Writing style

This is a marketing site for C-level executives, VPs, and decision-makers in organizations. Every word of copy must read as if a credible human professional wrote it, not an AI.

- **Never use em dashes (`—`).** They are a tell that AI generated the text and they cost us credibility. Use a period, a comma, a colon, parentheses, or rewrite the sentence instead. This applies to all user-facing copy and to en dashes used as sentence breaks too; reserve dashes only for genuine numeric ranges if needed.
- Keep the tone professional, direct, and confident. Write the way a respected operator or founder would speak to a buyer, not the way a chatbot pads an answer.
- Avoid AI-tell filler: no "in today's fast-paced world", no "unlock/unleash/elevate/supercharge", no "it's not just X, it's Y" constructions, no hedging throat-clearing. Make concrete claims.
- Prefer short, plain sentences over clever ones. Specificity beats adjectives.
